# Cleanroom Checklist

> **How to use:** Run this **before** you start any client, confidential, or sensitive work with an AI tool. It's a fast safety pass to make sure you're in the right account, using allowed data only, and not contaminating a client or your studio. Pairs with `/cleanroom`. Check every box or resolve it before proceeding. If any critical item can't be checked, stop and escalate. Not legal advice — align with your contracts and counsel.

## Files and data

- [ ] **Allowed files** identified: [list or "see project brief"]
- [ ] **Forbidden files** identified and kept out: [list — e.g. contracts, source masters, financials]
- [ ] **Client data** handling confirmed against the agreement (what may be processed, where)
- [ ] **Employee / contractor personal data** excluded unless explicitly permitted
- [ ] **Source code / source assets** handling confirmed (no proprietary code/assets sent to unapproved tools)

## AI tool behavior

- [ ] **Prompt logging** stance known (is this vendor/tier logging or training on inputs?)
- [ ] **Telemetry** stance known and acceptable for this work
- [ ] **Consent** in place where required (client aware AI is used, if the contract requires it)
- [ ] **GDPR / privacy** obligations considered for any personal data involved

## Contamination and export

- [ ] **Cross-contamination risk** checked (client A's data won't leak into client B's outputs or memory)
- [ ] **Export rules** clear (where outputs may be stored, shared, or shipped)
- [ ] **Safest workflow** chosen (e.g. redact first, use a restricted/local tier, or keep sensitive parts out of AI entirely)
