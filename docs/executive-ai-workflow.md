# Executive AI Workflow

**AI is not the job. AI is the leverage layer.**

Discipline is easier to describe than to keep. The way you keep it is by turning principles into a rhythm — a small set of moves you make at the same points every day and every week, until using AI well is a habit rather than a decision. This guide lays out that rhythm. It assumes you've read `ai-discipline-for-executives.md` and `survival-before-novelty.md`; here we make them operational.

The whole rhythm takes well under an hour a day of AI-specific overhead, and it replaces far more time than it costs.

## Daily rhythm

### Morning: plan and prioritize (10–15 min)

Start by orienting, not by doing. Pull up yesterday's `/wrapup` so you resume your real thread instead of reconstructing it from memory. Then decide the day's priorities *against the survival order* — run **`/survival-filter`** over your candidate work so the most fun task doesn't quietly outrank the most important one.

Capture the day's commitments in **`/task-ledger`**. The ledger is your single list of what you owe and what you're waiting on; without it, AI's endless suggestions become the agenda instead of your priorities.

**Output of the morning:** a short, ranked list of what actually matters today, with the survival level of each item clear in your head.

### Midday: spec and delegate (as work arises)

When a real piece of work surfaces, do not start building it. Write the spec with **`/spec-first`**: outcome, audience, definition of done, out-of-scope, and what would make it not worth doing. For anything with real time or money attached, run **`/roi-check`** on the spec before committing.

Then get it out of your hands. Route the work to the right tier with **`/model-router`** and hand implementation off with **`/handoff-to-cowork`**, carrying the spec, constraints, and deadline. Your name comes back to the work at review, not during the build.

**Output of the midday block:** specs written, work delegated, your hands free for the things only you can do.

### Afternoon: review and decide (as work returns)

Review delegated work *against its spec* — not against your mood, and not by re-building it yourself. Accept, redirect, or kill. When you make a decision of consequence — a direction, a tradeoff, a commitment — record it with **`/decision-log`**. The decision log is what stops the same question from being re-litigated next month and gives you a defensible record of why you chose what you chose.

**Output of the afternoon:** work reviewed against specs, decisions captured with their reasoning.

### End of day: wrap up (5–10 min)

Close the day with **`/wrapup`**: what was decided, what's still open, what happens next, and where each thread stands. Update **`/task-ledger`** so open items carry forward instead of falling through the interruption gaps that define executive work. Tomorrow's morning planning starts from this wrapup — that's what makes the days connect instead of resetting.

**Output of the day:** a resumable thread, not a scattered memory.

## Weekly rhythm

### Weekly review and re-rank (20–30 min)

Once a week, zoom out. Re-run **`/survival-filter`** at the portfolio level: given how the week went, which survival level needs your attention now? Cash secured for the quarter earns you room lower down the list; a wobbling client snaps delivery and revenue back to the top. Where money is involved, pressure-test the numbers with **`/forecast-check`** so next week's priorities rest on projections you'd actually bet on.

Review the week's **`/decision-log`** and **`/task-ledger`**: which decisions held, which need revisiting, what's stuck and why.

### Project-memory sync (15–20 min)

The most important weekly move for continuity: feed the week's wrapups, decisions, and synthesized meetings into **`/project-memory`**. This is where scattered daily context compounds into durable studio knowledge instead of evaporating. Use **`/meeting-synthesis`** to condense the week's meetings into decisions and actions before they go into memory, so what you retain is signal, not transcript.

Over months, `/project-memory` becomes the thing that lets you (and your team, and your AI sessions) start from everything the studio has already learned rather than from scratch.

## The rhythm at a glance

| When | Move | Skills |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Morning | Orient, prioritize by survival order | `/wrapup` (yesterday's), `/survival-filter`, `/task-ledger` |
| Midday | Spec, check ROI, route, delegate | `/spec-first`, `/roi-check`, `/model-router`, `/handoff-to-cowork` |
| Afternoon | Review against spec, log decisions | `/decision-log` |
| End of day | Close and carry forward | `/wrapup`, `/task-ledger` |
| Weekly | Re-rank, verify numbers, review | `/survival-filter`, `/forecast-check`, `/decision-log` |
| Weekly | Compound the context | `/meeting-synthesis`, `/project-memory` |

## Why the rhythm matters

Any single one of these moves is easy to skip on a given day, and skipping it rarely hurts immediately. The cost shows up later — as re-done thinking, re-litigated decisions, work done fast in the wrong direction, and context lost to interruption. The rhythm is insurance against exactly those slow, invisible leaks. Keep it for a month and the compounding is obvious: your days connect, your decisions stick, and AI stays what it should be — the leverage layer, not the job.
